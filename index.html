Academic Research

BA (Hons) Marketing Dissertation — First Class Honours
The Psychology of Advertising: Self-Monitoring & Purchase Intentions

This research explored how the personality trait of self-monitoring affects consumer purchase intentions towards different advertising styles — specifically self-enhancing (aspirational) versus self-deprecating (authentic) messaging.

Using a 2×2 factorial ANOVA design with 152 participants, the study found that self-monitoring significantly moderates advertising response. Low self-monitors showed meaningfully higher purchase intentions towards authentic, honest advertising — a finding with direct implications for brands in wellness and healthcare that rely on trust-based communication.

The research contributes to an underexplored area of consumer psychology, and the findings directly inform how I approach audience segmentation and message framing in my own campaigns.

Consumer Psychology Quantitative Research ANOVA Advertising Strategy First Class
H1 — Supported
Self-monitoring significantly moderates the effect of advertising style on purchase intentions. The interaction effect was confirmed through factorial ANOVA.
H1b — Supported
Low self-monitors showed significantly higher purchase intentions towards self-deprecating advertising (M=10.94) vs. self-enhancing (M=8.44). Medium effect size — Cohen's d = .596.
Marketing Implication
Authentic, performance-led messaging outperforms aspirational advertising for audiences who prioritise genuine product value — directly relevant for healthcare and wellness brands.
How It Shapes My Work
Message framing and audience psychology sit at the heart of how I approach content and campaign strategy — understanding who you're talking to is just as important as what you say.
